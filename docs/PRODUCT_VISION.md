# Product vision — Odyssey

**Read this before building features** so UI, content, and systems stay aligned with the expedition metaphor and long-term goals.

---

## One-liner

A step tracker where your avatar is a solo traveler ticking off the world’s most iconic trails: **Appalachian Trail**, **Camino de Santiago**, **Patagonia**, **Nepal**. Each region has its own visual biome, local companion animals (a llama in the Andes, a snow leopard in the Himalayas), and culturally inspired gear unlocks. The journey map becomes a literal world map you’ve colored in. Meeting fellow hikers on the same route makes it feel like you’re all on one expedition.

---

## Core experience pillars

### Trails & world

- **Regional biomes** — Distinct look and feel per trail / region.
- **Companion animals** — Region-specific (e.g. llama in the Andes, snow leopard in the Himalayas); some are **collectible pets** you meet and keep.
- **Gear** — Unlocks inspired by local culture and trail identity.
- **World map** — Progress “colors in” the map; completed trails feel like stamps on a globe.

### Social & async connection

- **Fellow hikers** — Others walking the same route feel like companions on the same expedition (not necessarily real-time multiplayer).
- **Stranger travelers** — You can **exchange emojis** sent **asynchronously** (no pressure for live chat).

### Collectibles & progression

- **Postcards** — One per **landmark** along a trail; a tangible “I was here” collection.
- **Passport** — Check off trails you’ve **completed**; **rubber-stamp** metaphor for finishing a route.
- **Curiosities** — Small finds on the trail: a smooth river stone, a feather, a coin, a folded note. Stored in a **curiosity collection** in your pack. **Rarity** tiers (common → extremely rare).

### Learning

- **Lore & factoids** — Interesting **history, culture, and trail facts** surfaced as you walk — educational without feeling like homework.

---

## What this doc is for

- **Engineering** — Feature scope and tone (expedition, not gym leaderboard).
- **Design** — Biomes, companions, passport/postcard metaphors.
- **Content** — Landmarks, copy, rarity tables for curiosities.

When in doubt, ask: *Does this make the player feel like a traveler on a real trail with others, not alone on a treadmill?*
